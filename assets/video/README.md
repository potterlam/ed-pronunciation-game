# Video Files Required

Place your CapCut videos in this folder.

## Required Videos

### 1. intro.mp4
**Purpose:** Introduction video that explains the game and pronunciation rules

**Suggested Content:**
- Welcome message (e.g., "Welcome to the Past Tense Adventure!")
- Brief story introduction (fantasy/adventure theme)
- Explanation of the three -ed pronunciation rules:
  - **/t/** - After voiceless sounds (p, k, f, s, sh, ch)
    - Examples: walked, helped, watched
  - **/d/** - After voiced sounds (b, g, v, z, l, m, n, r, vowels)
    - Examples: played, lived, cleaned
  - **/Éªd/** - After t or d sounds
    - Examples: wanted, needed, visited

**Recommended Length:** 1-3 minutes

**CapCut Tips:**
- Use animated text for the pronunciation rules
- Add visual examples of words
- Include background music
- Use transitions between sections
- Export at 1080p (1920x1080) for best quality

---

### 2. ending.mp4
**Purpose:** Congratulations video that wraps up the story

**Suggested Content:**
- Congratulations message
- Story conclusion/resolution
- Encouragement to practice more
- Invitation to play again
- Thank you message

**Recommended Length:** 1-2 minutes

**CapCut Tips:**
- Celebrate the player's achievement
- Show final score (optional - can be dynamic)
- Use upbeat music
- Add confetti or celebration effects
- Export at 1080p (1920x1080)

---

## Technical Requirements

### Format
- Container: MP4
- Video Codec: H.264 (most compatible)
- Audio Codec: AAC

### Resolution
- Recommended: 1920x1080 (1080p)
- Minimum: 1280x720 (720p)
- Aspect Ratio: 16:9

### Quality Settings
- Bitrate: 5-10 Mbps for video
- Frame Rate: 30 fps or 60 fps
- Audio: 128 kbps or higher

### File Size
- Try to keep under 50MB each for faster loading
- Compress if needed while maintaining quality

---

## CapCut Export Settings

1. Open your project in CapCut
2. Click "Export"
3. Settings:
   - Resolution: 1080p
   - Frame rate: 30 FPS
   - Format: MP4
   - Quality: High or Smart HDR
4. Export and save as `intro.mp4` or `ending.mp4`

## Creating Videos in CapCut

### For Intro Video

**Template Structure:**
1. **Opening Scene** (5-10 sec)
   - Title: "Past Tense -ED Adventure"
   - Subtitle: "Learn pronunciation the fun way!"

2. **Story Introduction** (15-20 sec)
   - Brief fantasy/adventure story setup
   - Characters or theme introduction

3. **Rule 1: /t/** (20 sec)
   - Animation showing the rule
   - Examples: walked, helped, watched
   - Visual: Highlight the voiceless ending sounds

4. **Rule 2: /d/** (20 sec)
   - Animation showing the rule
   - Examples: played, cleaned, lived
   - Visual: Highlight the voiced ending sounds

5. **Rule 3: /Éªd/** (20 sec)
   - Animation showing the rule
   - Examples: wanted, needed, visited
   - Visual: Highlight t/d before -ed

6. **Closing** (10 sec)
   - "Ready to start? Let's go!"
   - Transition to game start

**Design Elements:**
- Colorful backgrounds
- Animated text
- Icons or emoji
- Background music (upbeat, educational)
- Sound effects for emphasis

---

### For Ending Video

**Template Structure:**
1. **Congratulations** (5-10 sec)
   - "Congratulations!"
   - Celebration animation

2. **Story Conclusion** (15-20 sec)
   - Wrap up the adventure story
   - Show achievement

3. **Encouragement** (15-20 sec)
   - "You've mastered -ed pronunciation!"
   - "Keep practicing!"

4. **Call to Action** (10 sec)
   - "Play again to improve your score!"
   - "Challenge your friends!"

**Design Elements:**
- Confetti/fireworks effects
- Trophy or medal graphics
- Cheerful music
- Bright, celebratory colors

## Alternative: Simple Text Videos

If you don't have time for elaborate videos, you can create simple text-based videos:

1. **Static Background** + **Animated Text**
   - Choose a colorful gradient background
   - Add text animations for each rule
   - Duration: 30-60 seconds each

2. **Slideshow Style**
   - Create slides with rules and examples
   - Add transitions between slides
   - Background music

## Optional: No Videos?

If you don't have videos yet, the game will still work! The video elements will show a message about unsupported video. You can:

1. **Temporarily:** Add placeholder text instead of videos
2. **Later:** Create and add videos when ready
3. **Alternative:** Remove video sections from HTML if not needed
